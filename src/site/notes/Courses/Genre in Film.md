> [!INFO|float-right-medium] Catalog description
> Studies of the aesthetic, ideological, and historical dimensions of film genre(s) such as film noir, horror, romantic comedy, the musical, etc, with examples drawn from global film industries. Topics will vary by semester and may include a comparative approach to genre.

**Genre in Film** is an intermediate-level film course I created for the English Department at TCU. I taught the first instance of this course in Fall 2024, with a [[Folk horror\|folk horror]] theme. In the future, I hope to teach the course with a [[film noir\|film noir]] theme. 

The course is designed to work alongside other film offerings in the English Department, including the introductory-level [[Courses/Approaches to Film\|Approaches to Film]], upper-level [[Theories of Cinema\|Theories of Cinema]] and [[Literature and Film\|Literature and Film]], and the practicum-style [[Multimedia Authoring—Animation & Film\|Multimedia Authoring—Animation & Film]]. I also plan to create an additional intermediate-level course, [[Movements in Film\|Movements in Film]], thus significantly expanding the number of film courses students can take in the English Department.
